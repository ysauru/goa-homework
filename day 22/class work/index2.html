Salvador Dalí

The Master of Surrealism

Salvador Dalí (1904–1989) was a Spanish surrealist artist known for his striking and bizarre images. His best-known work,The Persistence of Memory features melting clocks in a dreamlike landscape.

Famous Works

The Persistence of Memory (1931) - Dalí's most iconic painting.

Swans Reflecting Elephants (1937) - A great example of Dalí's surrealist style.

Fun Facts

Dalí had a flamboyant mustache that became his trademark, He collaborated with Walt Disney on a short animated film, Dalí claimed he got his ideas from his dreams.
