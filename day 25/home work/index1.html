Rock music

What is Rock? Key Eras Subgenres 
Rock music is a genre that evolved in the 1950s,
rooted in blues, country, and rhythm & blues. 
It's usually driven by electric guitars, strong rhythms, bass, and drums. 
Vocals range from melodic to straight-up screaming depending on the subgenre.

Key eras and styles 

1950s – Birth of Rock 'n' Roll

    Artists: Chuck Berry, Little Richard, Elvis Presley
    
    Catchy riffs, upbeat tempos, and early rebellion vibes.
    
1960s – The British Invasion & Psychedelia
    
    Artists: The Beatles, The Rolling Stones, Jimi Hendrix, The Doors
    
    British bands dominate, and things get more experimental and trippy.
    
1970s – Hard Rock & Punk
     
    Artists: Led Zeppelin, Queen, Pink Floyd, The Ramones, Sex Pistols
    
    Big riffs, guitar solos, and raw energy. Prog rock gets complex, punk strips it all down.
    
1980s – Glam, Metal, and Alternative
    
    Artists: Guns N’ Roses, Metallica, U2, The Cure
    
    Big hair, big solos, stadium anthems. Alt-rock and indie scenes begin to rise.
    
1990s – Grunge & Alternative Boom
    
    Artists: Nirvana, Pearl Jam, Radiohead, Green Day
    
     Gritty, emotional, and real. Grunge kills glam, and alternative goes mainstream.
    
2000s–Now – Genre Mixing & Revival
Artists: The White Stripes, Muse, Arctic Monkeys, Foo Fighters, Paramore Rock blends with indie, pop, and electronic. Retro sounds make comebacks.

subgenres

  • Classic Rock – Timeless anthems from the '60s–'80s
  • Hard Rock – Loud, aggressive guitar-driven rock
  • Punk Rock – Fast, raw, anti-establishment
  • Alternative Rock – Catch-all for non-mainstream styles
  • Indie Rock – Independent, often experimental and emotional
  • Grunge – Dark, fuzzy, angsty (Seattle scene)
