More albums

  • In Rainbows (2007) The Beautiful One. Emotionally rich, sonically warm, and full of groove. In Rainbows feels alive and intimate — less anxious than their previous work, but no less deep. Why it’s beloved: It’s accessible and profound. Fans often call this their most “human” album. Essential tracks: “Weird Fishes/Arpeggi,” “Nude,” “Reckoner,” “All I Need”
  • The Bends (1995) A huge leap forward. More complex songwriting, emotional depth. Big songs: “Fake Plastic Trees”, “High and Dry”, “Street Spirit (Fade Out)”. Mixed vulnerability and guitar-driven intensity. Critics and fans took notice.
  • Kid A (2000) The real curveball. No radio singles, hardly any guitars. Heavily electronic, abstract, influenced by Aphex Twin, krautrock, ambient, and jazz. Fans were shocked at first — now it's considered a masterpiece. Key tracks: “Everything In Its Right Place”, “How to Disappear Completely”, “Idioteque”.
